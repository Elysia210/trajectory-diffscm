## Thought process / method evolution

### A. First question: is step guidance actually working, or was it a one-off?

The first priority this week was not adding more complexity, but checking whether the main step-delta guided result survives:

- different random seeds,
- different guidance scales,
- repeated sampling runs.

Why:

- A single visually good run is easy to over-trust.
- The key claim is not just "guidance can move risk", but "guidance moves risk reliably without immediate trajectory collapse".

Decision:

- Lock the representation to step-delta future motion.
- Keep the classifier-guided sampler simple.
- Run a seed sweep before spending more time on fancy guidance variants.

### B. Second question: can we keep the risk effect while reducing motion distortion?

After vanilla guidance looked real, the next concern was realism:

- collision probability could be pushed up,
- but guided trajectories still drifted away from the real motion distribution.

Decision:

- Add a preservation term against an unguided reference rollout generated from the same noise.
- Keep the preservation target local in step space instead of only looking at endpoints.
- Separate the treatment of the two gradients:
  - risk gradient should be normalized/masked/clipped,
  - preservation gradient should keep more of its raw magnitude structure.

### C. Third question: if preservation fails, make the failure inspectable

The preservation branch did not immediately behave well, so the work shifted from "ship the new method" to "make failure modes measurable".

Decision:

- Save comparison plots and motion summaries for every preservation variant.
- Keep the failed runs instead of deleting them.
- Use those runs to diagnose whether instability comes from:
  - risk gradient scale,
  - preservation weight,
  - adversary emphasis,
  - step-gradient clipping.

## Main results

### 1. Vanilla step guidance was confirmed

From `results/weekly_2026-04-23_step_guidance_confirm/tables/mode_aggregate_summary.csv`:

- `no_collision_scale1p0` mean collision probability: `0.1618`
- `unguided` mean collision probability: `0.1857`
- `collision_scale0p5`: `0.2103`
- `collision_scale1p0`: `0.2197`
- `collision_scale2p0`: `0.2376`
- `collision_scale3p0`: `0.2672`

From `results/weekly_2026-04-23_step_guidance_confirm/tables/ordering_checks.json`:

- all four seeds satisfy `no_collision < unguided < collision_s2.0`
- all four seeds also satisfy `no_collision < unguided < collision_s1.0`
- collision-guided probability increases monotonically from scale `0.5` to `3.0`

Interpretation:

- The main guidance effect is real, stable, and not limited to one seed.
- Increasing collision guidance scale raises classifier risk in a controlled, monotonic way.

### 2. Motion realism under vanilla guidance stayed in roughly the same range

Using the same aggregate table:

- Ego endpoint displacement decreases from `54.60` in unguided to `50.88` at collision scale `2.0`, then `49.11` at scale `3.0`.
- Adversary endpoint displacement stays in a narrower band, from `35.85` unguided to `37.05` at scale `3.0`.
- Step statistics move, but do not show catastrophic explosion in the vanilla multi-seed runs.

Interpretation:

- Vanilla guidance is not perfectly realistic.
- But the multi-seed confirmation does not show the kind of severe blow-up that would invalidate the method immediately.

### 3. The first preservation-guided attempt failed badly

From `results/weekly_2026-04-23_step_guidance_confirm/tables/seed123_vanilla_vs_preservation.json` and `tables/preservation_motion_summary.json`:

- seed-123 vanilla collision `s2.0` raises mean risk from `0.1932` to `0.2239`
- the first preservation-guided run reaches `0.2656`
- but motion metrics explode:
  - ego step mean around `1.38e5`
  - ego endpoint displacement around `1.60e6`
  - adversary step mean around `8.46e3`

Interpretation:

- The preservation idea did not fail by "doing nothing".
- It failed by introducing unstable gradients that can push sampling completely off the physical manifold.

### 4. Later preservation variants reduced the explosion but still did not solve realism

Representative numbers:

- `preservation_v1_l005`:
  - ego step mean `1.2116` vs real `0.8454`
  - ego step max `2.7895` vs real `0.9334`
- `preservation_v2_l010`:
  - ego step mean `1.2769` vs real `0.8454`
  - ego step max `2.7035` vs real `0.9334`
  - adv step mean `0.7663` vs real `0.4261`

Interpretation:

- The later variants are far more stable than the first failed preservation run.
- But they still do not recover real-motion statistics closely enough to call the preservation problem solved.

## Current conclusion

### What is confirmed

- Step-delta classifier guidance works.
- The main effect is reproducible across seeds.
- Collision guidance strength produces monotonic risk movement.
- Vanilla guided sampling is currently the strongest validated result in this repo.

### What is not solved yet

- Preservation-aware guidance is not stable enough yet.
- Even the improved preservation variants still show noticeably inflated motion statistics relative to real trajectories.
- The preservation branch needs another round of gradient design or objective redesign before it can replace vanilla guidance.

## Suggested external-facing summary

This week we focused on validating trajectory-domain guidance rather than only adding new components. We confirmed across four random seeds that the step-delta diffusion model can be steered by the collision classifier in a stable and monotonic way: no-collision guidance lowers predicted risk, collision guidance raises it, and stronger collision guidance increases risk consistently. We then explored a preservation-aware extension to keep guided samples closer to natural motion, but the first version was unstable and later variants, while much better, still did not match real-motion statistics closely enough. The practical outcome is that vanilla step guidance is now a credible baseline, and preservation-aware guidance remains an active optimization problem rather than a finished method.
